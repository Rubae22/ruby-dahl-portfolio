Emotorise

Project

Typography in Context (ANU)

Medium

Adobe Illustrator

Year

2025

Role

Concept, type design, sampler & mockups

This was a collaborative project to design Emotorise, a display typeface inspired by Japanese typographic systems, Showa-era graphic design, and contemporary pop culture. The brief simulated a real-world type foundry, designing, developing, and marketing a complete typeface system through standard foundry outputs.

Emotorise is built on a strict 6×6 grid with uniform strokes and deliberate spatial breaks that reference Japanese Jihaba and Tategaki structures. Drawing on kawaii culture and internet emoticons, its modular characters are designed to work as both letters and expressive graphic symbols.

The final system spans a full type specimen and promotional material: a functional yet expressive typeface that blends structured grid logic with pop-cultural visual language, positioned within both historical and contemporary typographic contexts.

My Role
  • Co-originated the concept: the Japanese-inspired, emoticon-driven direction.
  • Drew every initial sketch and set the grid the letterforms were built on.
  • Ran the weekly feedback loop, redrawing the face into its final 6×6 stepped grid.
  • Took the digital build back to spec when it drifted from the approved sketches.
  • Designed the complete type sampler.
  • Produced all visual mockups: poster sit-ins, digital, and zines.
  • Wrote and designed the final rationale solo.
