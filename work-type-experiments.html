Type Experiments

Project

Typography in Context (ANU)

Medium

Adobe Illustrator

Year

2025

A series of three typographic experiments exploring blackletter as a consistent visual system across different scales and contexts. I moved away from graffiti as a primary influence to push my typography into more structured, historical reference points, developing my own ornamental uppercase letterforms alongside blackletter typefaces sourced from Adobe Fonts.

Small Experiment / Martyr

I focused on the word Martyr to capture the heaviness it carries, its biblical and cultural weight, and set that against the highly ornamental way blackletter capitals were historically crafted. I designed a custom ornamental M on a blackletter grid, digitised it and paired it with the typeface Sabbath Black, then printed the design and moulded the letters into a raised surface. The result gives the word a literal, physical weight and a real presence in three dimensions, letting the ornamentation elevate the word while exposing how heavy it is, both visually and conceptually.

Medium Experiment / Blackletter & Graffiti

The second experiment explored the overlap between blackletter and graffiti through Cholo-style lettering, a hand-penned script that grew out of Old English and blackletter forms in mid-century Los Angeles. I layered sacred text, Psalm 23:4, with Coolio's reworking of the same passage in Gangsta's Paradise, hand-writing the blackletter with a chisel-tip marker and rendering the lyric half in a Cholo-inspired graffiti style. It became a study in translating language, style, medium and cultural meaning across one composition.

Large Experiment / Collage Poster

The large-scale experiment drew on David Carson's fragmented, layered poster work. Using lyrics from Bladee's One in a Million for their religious undertones, which tie back to blackletter's historic link to scripture, I reimagined the style not as rigid historical script but as something chaotic and collage-like. Distorting, overlapping and re-weighting words like tragic, million and sinned built hierarchy and depth, freeing blackletter into a more fluid and expressive direction.

Across all three outcomes, the project investigates how blackletter can shift between historical reference, material exploration and contemporary typographic expression while holding a consistent visual language.
