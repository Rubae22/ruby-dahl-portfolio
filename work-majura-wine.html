Mount Majura

Project

Visual Branding and Identity Systems (UC)

Medium

Adobe Illustrator

Year

2026

This was a client-led packaging design project for Mount Majura Vineyard, presented to my class as a live competition brief that each of us worked on individually. The task was to develop a label system for a new vermouth range, simulating a real-world branding commission and requiring consideration of concept development, visual identity, and Australian alcohol labelling regulations. My outcome was a three-label system for different vermouth expressions, designed as a cohesive product range. The concept combined Art Nouveau-inspired botanical illustration with Art Deco typography to reference the historical origins of vermouth while positioning the brand within a contemporary Australian context.

Each variant was differentiated through ingredient-led illustration and colour, while maintaining consistency through a shared typographic and grid system to ensure brand cohesion across the range. Regulatory information was integrated into the layout to meet Australian labelling requirements without disrupting visual hierarchy. The result was print-ready label designs, mockups, and a supporting brand system demonstrating how the identity could extend across future products.

Throughout the project we worked closely with the team at Mount Majura Vineyard, getting regular feedback from them that shaped the direction of the work. Final selection in the competition is yet to be announced.
